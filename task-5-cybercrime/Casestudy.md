# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing

Phishing is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted organization. These attacks usually occur through emails, messages, or fake websites that look genuine.

A typical phishing attack begins when a student receives an email or SMS that appears to be from a bank, online shopping site, or college administration. The message often creates urgency, such as “Your account will be blocked” or “Verify your details immediately.” It includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look like the original.

When the victim enters their login credentials or banking details on this fake site, the attacker captures the information. The scammer can then access the victim’s account, transfer money, or misuse personal data.

Students are frequent targets because they regularly use emails, online services, and digital payments. Many may not notice small differences in fake links or email addresses.

The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and compromised accounts. In some cases, victims may also lose access to their important data.

This case highlights the importance of checking website URLs, avoiding suspicious links, and never sharing sensitive information online. Awareness is the best defense against phishing attacks.
