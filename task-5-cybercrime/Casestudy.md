#  **Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness**  
##  **Part A – Case Study: Phishing Attack**

###  **Introduction**
**Phishing** is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

---

###  **Case Description**
A student received an email claiming to be from a bank asking to verify account details urgently. The email contained a link that looked like the official bank website. The student clicked the link and entered login credentials and OTP.

Later, the student discovered that money had been deducted from their bank account without authorization. The email was fake, and the website was a phishing site designed to steal information.

---

###  **Problem Identified**
- **Fake email pretending to be a trusted organization**  
- **Suspicious link leading to a fraudulent website**  
- **Lack of verification before sharing sensitive information**  

---

### **Preventive Measures**
- **Always check the sender’s email address carefully**  
- **Avoid clicking on unknown or suspicious links**  
- **Never share OTPs, passwords, or bank details online**  
- **Use official websites by typing the URL manually**  
- **Enable two-factor authentication for extra security**  

---

###  **Conclusion**
**Phishing attacks are common but can be prevented with awareness and careful online behavior. Users should always verify information before taking action to stay safe in the digital world.**
